üß¨ What is gel electrophoresis?

Gel electrophoresis is a laboratory technique used to separate DNA, RNA, or protein molecules based on their size and electrical charge. The molecules are placed in a gel matrix and an electric current is applied, causing them to migrate through the gel at different rates.

Our platform uses advanced computer vision algorithms to automatically detect and analyze DNA bands in gel electrophoresis images. It combines OpenCV image processing with machine learning pattern recognition to provide accurate disease predictions and forensic analysis.

üîç Forensic Analysis

üìà What is the match score?

The match score is a percentage (0-100%) that indicates the similarity between two DNA samples. It's calculated based on band count similarity, band position alignment, and pattern matching. Higher scores indicate greater similarity.

The forensic analysis provides statistical confidence levels and similarity scores. However, it should be used as a preliminary screening tool and not as definitive evidence. Professional forensic analysis should be conducted for legal purposes.
